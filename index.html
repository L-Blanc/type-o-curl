Type 1

Type 1 hair is naturally straight with no defined curl pattern. This type of hair requires a lot of heat to curl and heavy products to keep styles in place. Type 1 hair also reflects the most shine as hair oils can travel straight from scalp to tip.

Type 2

2 / 8
Type 2 hair has a bit more of a natural wave to it than type 1 hair. It's not completley straight buuuuut not necesarrily curly either. Type 2 has some bounce and isn't overly oily or dry. Light sprays and mouse should get the job done for simple styles.

Type 3a

3 / 8
Type 3a hair has a natural definite curl pattern and is typically more voluminous and patterned than type 2. These curls are s-shaped and form ringlets that make it hard for natural oils to travel all the way down the shaft, often leading to dry hair.

Type 3b

4 / 8
Type 3b curls have more spring and spirals which requrie more moisture and oils to be added to them than type 3a.

Type 3c

5 / 8
Type 3c curls are more tightly packed than type 3b and are the most voluminous of type 3 hair. The curls are about pencil width and corkscrew like. They tend to dry fast so hydration and moisture are key!

Type 4a

6 / 8
Type 4a curls are wiry and start to resemble more coils than ringlet-like curls like in type 3 hair. The individual hair strands have a dense s-shape that make it hard for oils to travel down the shaft making type 4 hair the driest of all types.

Type 4b

7 / 8
Rather than curling or coiling, Type 4b curls are bent at an angle. These z-shaped curls are fragile and prone to shriinkage.

Type 4c

8 / 8
Type 4c curls are as densely packed but with less definition than the other type 4 curls. These curls can range from fine to wiry to coarse and require the most product for styling.

Low Porosity

If you have low porosity hair, your hair cuitcles lay down flat on top of one another and initally repel moisture until they open up and allow water to pass. Then cuticles close back up and once that moisture is in there its not going anywhere! These strands are prone to product build up which can leave the hair feeling dry or sticky, so try and do a clease or co-wash a few days before wash day. Use warm water and deep conditioner treatments with heat to open up the cuiticle and help moisture flow more freely.

Medium Porosity

For medium porosity hair, the cuticle is looser and lets in just the right amouht of water to hydrate your hair and hold styles for a good amount of time. This porosity is also great at holding on to color and other chemical processes. Occasional deep conditioning is good especiialy when the hair feels drier.

High Porosity

If you have high porosity hair, the cuticle is wide open with gaps inbetween that let in a lot of moisture which can lead to frizz and tangling. High porosity hair dries quickly as it lets water out as quickly as it comes in, so use cold water and oils to seal in moisture.
